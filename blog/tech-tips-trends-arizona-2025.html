Smart Tips and Tricks for Everyday Devices Life is busy—little shortcuts can make technology feel like less of a chore. National tech advisor Kim Komando (who broadcasts from Phoenix) shared some no-nonsense tips for 2025 komando.com . Here are a few favorites: Undo almost anything – Press Ctrl + Z (or Cmd + Z on a Mac) to undo mistakes beyond Word documents: un-send an email, reopen a closed tab or reverse file changes komando.com . Take screen captures – Use Windows key + Shift + S on a PC or Cmd + Shift + 4 on a Mac to quickly capture recipes, directions or error messages komando.com . Tame your tabs – The free OneTab browser extension converts messy browser windows into a neat list to save memory and sanity komando.com . Use your voice – Siri and Google Assistant can open apps, call friends, set reminders and even convert measurements komando.com . It’s faster than hunting through menus. Keep track of kids and keys – Small Bluetooth trackers like Apple AirTags or Tile can attach to backpacks or keyrings so you always know where things are komando.com . Schedule emails – Apple Mail and Gmail now let you draft messages and schedule them for a specific date and time komando.com . Automate routine tasks – Smart home routines (e.g., “Good night” turns off lights and locks doors) and automation apps like IFTTT or Apple’s Shortcuts can handle repetitive chores komando.com . Explore AI assistants – Tools like ChatGPT or Perplexity can plan meals, write speeches or analyze industry trends komando.com . They’re powerful helpers—but use them responsibly. Cybersecurity Essentials: Staying Safe Online Protecting yourself online is more important than ever. Experts at Security.org compiled 25 practical tips to reduce your risk of hacking security.org . Here are key takeaways: Keep software updated – Install operating system and app updates promptly; they contain important security patches security.org . Use antivirus software where appropriate – Windows PCs and Macs benefit from reputable antivirus tools security.org . Practice strong password hygiene – Use unique passwords (12–16 characters with letters, numbers and symbols), avoid personal info, and store them in a password manager security.org . Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) – Add an extra layer of protection by requiring a code or biometric confirmation security.org . Beware of phishing – Don’t click on suspicious emails, texts or attachments. Phishing scams can steal your credentials security.org . Limit sharing of personal information – Protect personally identifiable information (PII) like your address, phone number or Social Security number security.org . Secure your mobile devices – Use passcodes or biometrics, disable public Wi-Fi and location services when not needed, and keep your device updated security.org . By following these steps and asking for help when you’re unsure, you reduce the risk of identity theft, malware and cyber-attacks. At Desert Signal IT, we also recommend using a reliable VPN when connecting to public Wi-Fi and enabling automatic backups.
