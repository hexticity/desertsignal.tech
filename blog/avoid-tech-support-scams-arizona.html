How to Avoid Tech Support Scams in Arizona

Posted in Security · Arizona · 2025

If you’ve ever seen a scary pop-up saying “Your computer is infected, call this number now,” you’ve already met the most common tech support scam. These scams target Arizona residents through phone calls, emails, and fake websites.

Red flags to watch for

  • Unsolicited calls: Microsoft, Apple, or “your internet provider” will not call you out of the blue to fix your computer.
  • Urgent scare tactics: Messages that say “act now or lose everything” are almost always scams.
  • Requests for remote access: Scammers want to take control of your computer so they can plant problems or steal data.
  • Payment in gift cards or Zelle: Legitimate businesses don’t demand payment this way for tech support.

What to do if you see a scam pop-up

  • Do not call the phone number on the screen.
  • Try closing the browser tab or the entire browser.
  • If it won’t close, restart the computer.
  • After restarting, run your usual antivirus or security software.

If someone is already connected to your computer

If you’ve let someone remote into your computer and you’re now suspicious:

  • Immediately disconnect from the internet (unplug Ethernet or turn off Wi-Fi).
  • Shut the computer down fully.
  • Call a trusted local technician (like Desert Signal IT) or bring the device in for a checkup.

How to protect yourself going forward

  • Talk with family members (especially elders) about these scams.
  • Save the real support numbers for your ISP and trusted tech person.
  • Use a password manager and avoid reusing passwords.
  • Turn on two-factor authentication for important accounts.

You don’t have to figure this out alone. If something ever feels off, hang up, take a breath, and reach out to someone you trust before doing anything.
